In Short

I am a builder of meaningful solutions. I left my job assessing noise/chemical exposures in November of 2012 and taught myself Android. I realized I need and want to work on a team, so I signed up for Dev Bootcamp. I am now equipped to contribute as a full-stack web or Android developer.

Build Things

As a kid, I once built a very tall car from K'nex. Being tall is cool, but the height also served a purpose: potential energy. I couldn't have defined the term at the time, but I knew I could put a heavy object up high, turn the string it was hanging on around with a pulley, and wrap it around an axle. The vehicle moved on it's own. Giving behavior to inanimate objects is Fun.

Things that Matter

For over 5 years I supported people with developmental disabilities and mental illnesses. Service to others is not an alltruistic goal or ideal -- it's simply something I enjoy. I eventually felt there was more I could do, so I set out to become a doctor.

Things that are Challenging

I taught myself calculus after not using algebra or trigonometry for over 3 years. I took chemistry, physics, and biology for majors in one semester. I earned a score on the MCAT which is better than most doctors. It still didn't work out, and I moved on.

Builds that Matter

After college, I worked assessing noise and chemical exposures supported by software which could use UX redesign. I built a simple script to automate some data entry, and my coworkers were impressed and relieved. It had it's own behavior, it mattered, but it wasn't all that challenging.

Challenging Builds that Matter

I built an Android app which enables on-the-go planning of air sampling. It works, it matters, and it was challenging, but getting the solution into users' hands involves much more than just building it.

Build With Friends

I want to see a product I helped build in users' hands. That goal is best achieved as part of a team. At Dev Bootcamp I've gotten excellent suppport in developing further technical skills, but more importantly I've practiced and refined my ability to communicate. I loved the collaborative culture and practices at DBC.

The Rest of the Story

I'm looking for opportunities for further collaboration and continuous learning. I'd love to talk more! The world is filled with interesting projects. What's yours?
